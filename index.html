Why remote EEG matters

Seizures and epileptiform activity are common in hospitalized patients—especially in the ICU—and can be difficult to recognize clinically. EEG monitoring helps teams detect seizures (including nonconvulsive events), clarify encephalopathy patterns, and guide treatment decisions.

Supports higher-level inpatient care

Access to EEG expertise strengthens hospital-level neurology care by enabling timely diagnosis and management—without needing to transfer patients solely for EEG interpretation availability.

  • Improves access to specialized interpretation
  • Enables consistent coverage during staffing gaps
  • Helps standardize reporting and communication

Helps prevent unnecessary transfers

Remote interpretation can help centers provide advanced EEG monitoring locally, keeping patients closer to home and reducing delays that occur when transfers are required for monitoring or interpretation capacity.

  • Maintains continuity for families and referring clinicians
  • Reduces transfer-related delays and costs
  • Supports ICU/NICU decision making in real time

Clinically actionable results

EEG interpretation is most valuable when it’s clear and relevant to management. We emphasize structured impressions, correlation with clinical context, and practical recommendations aligned with your team’s needs.

  • Seizure and status epilepticus detection
  • Encephalopathy characterization
  • Medication stewardship and escalation decisions
In short: Remote EEG interpretation expands access to expert neurophysiology, strengthens local hospital capability, and can reduce transfers that are driven primarily by limited EEG coverage.
